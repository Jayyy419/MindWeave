# Pitch Video Improvement Draft (3 Minutes)

This draft aligns with challenge requirements:

- 3-minute YouTube presentation
- English narration
- Native-language subtitles allowed
- Simple and visual for audiences with limited AI knowledge

## Target Structure (180 seconds)

1. 0:00-0:25 - Problem hook and context
2. 0:25-0:50 - Beneficiaries and why this matters
3. 0:50-1:40 - Solution walkthrough with AI and wireframes
4. 1:40-2:15 - Public good and measurable impact
5. 2:15-2:45 - Outreach plan to raise AI awareness (1,000+)
6. 2:45-3:00 - Inspiration, innovation, and closing call

## Scene-by-Scene Script Guide

## Scene 1 (0:00-0:25) - Real Challenge

Visuals:
- ASEAN map + youth stress context overlays
- One short user scenario

Narration:
"Across ASEAN, many young people experience daily emotional pressure but do not have consistent access to practical support. MindWeave helps them reflect, learn coping strategies, and take healthier next steps."

## Scene 2 (0:25-0:50) - Beneficiaries

Visuals:
- Target persona cards
- School/community context

Narration:
"Our primary beneficiaries are youth aged [X-Y], especially students and early-career young adults facing stress, uncertainty, and limited support channels."

## Scene 3 (0:50-1:40) - AI Solution Explained Simply

Visuals:
- Product flow wireframes
- Input -> AI processing -> output
- Learning Library popup flow and interaction

Narration:
"MindWeave uses AI to help users reframe negative thoughts into constructive actions. It also provides structured learning modules with guided reflection, quizzes, and decision exercises. AI is paired with safety checks and support guidance for responsible use."

## Scene 4 (1:40-2:15) - Public Good and Measurable Impact

Visuals:
- Dashboard mockups or real analytics snapshots
- KPI trend lines

Narration:
"We measure impact through completion rates, retention, and user-reported improvement in coping confidence. Our pilot targets include [X] users, [Y]% lesson completion, and measurable positive changes over [Z] weeks."

## Scene 5 (2:15-2:45) - Outreach Plan (1,000+)

Visuals:
- Outreach funnel graphic
- Workshop photos/mockups
- QR onboarding flow

Narration:
"Our outreach combines campus workshops, community partners, youth ambassadors, and digital campaigns to reach at least 1,000 community members. Every channel is measured through referral and participation tracking."

## Scene 6 (2:45-3:00) - Why MindWeave Is Inspiring

Visuals:
- Impact montage
- Team mission statement

Narration:
"MindWeave is built to make emotional support practical, affordable, and culturally relevant across ASEAN. We are not only building an app, we are building a movement for responsible AI and healthier communities."

## Visual Checklist

- Keep text large and readable.
- Use clear icons and minimal jargon.
- Show actual product screens, not only slides.
- Include subtitles for accessibility.
- Use one consistent visual theme and color system.

## Rubric Mapping in the Video

- Problem definition (10%): Scene 1
- Relevancy and impact (10%): Scenes 2 and 4
- AI application (15%): Scene 3
- Viability (10%): Scenes 3 and 4
- Innovation (10%): Scenes 3 and 6
- Sustainability (10%): Scenes 4 and 5
- Outreach practicality/effectiveness (15% total outreach): Scene 5

## Production Tips

- Keep pace at 135-150 spoken words per minute.
- Avoid overlong intros.
- Use one sentence per visual beat.
- End with one clear impact statement and one call to action.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Spending too long on team introductions.
- Explaining AI with technical jargon.
- Showing no measurable outcomes.
- Mentioning outreach without numbers.
- Using abstract claims without product visuals.
